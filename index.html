Optional/Personal Info

Growing up on a farm, I learned the value of utility. If something wasn't useful, it was thrown out to make room for something that we needed more. This has greatly influenced my design style. I try and make sure that each element of a design has a specific thought process and purpose behind it. If it doesn't help to communicate the message I'm trying to send, I delete it. I would describe my personal style as minimalist, illustrative and grid-based. I am deadline oriented and excel at presenting my work.

Major

Visual Communication Design

I am currently beginning my last year at Eastern Washington University in pursuit of a degree in Visual Communication Design (VCD). I've always had a passion for organization. It sounds strange, I know, but even as a little kid I was rearranging my toys and bookshelves. This is what drew me to my current major. I've never considered myself much of an artist, but I really enjoy organizing visual content. In the book How to Think Like a Graphic Designer by Debbie Millman, Paula Scher defines graphic design as, "the art of planning." This is how I view my major and my chosen career field.

Professional Objectives

I hope to work creating visual representations of data, creating brands, or utilizing my growing interest in web design. I want to grow as a designer, both individually and in successfully fulfilling my roles in group situations. I want to continue to refine and streamline my design process. Most of all, I want to create work that both I and my future clients can be proud of.
